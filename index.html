Lemmy Kilmister

ROCK LEGEND

  • 1945 Ian Fraser Kilmister born in Stoke-on-Trent, United Kingdom.
  • 1960 Joined local bands “The rainmakers” and then “The Motown sect” who played in northern clubs for three years.
  • 1965 Joined “The Rockin’ Vickers” who signed a deal with CBS, released threee singles, and toured Europe.
  • 1967 Move to London shared a flat with Noel Redding, bassist of “The Jimi Hendrix Experience” and got a job has a roadie for the band.
  • 1968 He joined the psychedelic rock band “Sam Gopal” under the name of Ian Willis and recorderd the album “Escalator” wich was realeased in 1969.
  • 1969 Joins Opal Butterfly, nut the band soon disbanded, having to failed to raise enough interest with their singles.
  • 1971 Lemmy auditioned for the space rock band “hawkwind” but the band decides that they not need another guitarrist. By chance, the player bass doesn’t shows up so he took his place and became the basisst. Lemmy provided a distinctive bass work sound during his tenure and also lead vocals on several songs included band’s biggest UK chart single, “silver machine”, which rated #3 in 1972.
  • 1975 During North America tour, he was arrested for drug possesion. He was released without charge, but the band decided to fire him.
    • After hawkwind, Lemmy formed a new band called “bastards”, with guitarrist Larry Wallis and drummer Lucas Fox. Their manager told him that the band name would never get a slot on “Top of the Pops”. So Lemmy changed the band’s name to Mothorhead, the title of the last song he had written for Hawkwind.
    • Wallis and fox where replaced with “fast” Eddie Clarke as drummer and Phil “Philty Animal” Taylor, and with this line-up, the band began to achieve success.
  • 1980 - 1981 Mothorhead peaked with several UK hits, including the single “Ace of Spades” and the UK#1 live album “No sleep ‘til Hammersmith”.
  • 2003 Motorhead realeases their compilatory album “Hellriser: Best Epic Years” including colaboration with artists like: Ozzy Osbourne, Zakk Wylde, Ted Nugent, Slash and others.
  • 2005 UK magazine Classic Rock presented Lemmy with its first “Living Legend” award.
    • In this year, Motorhead won their first Grammy in the Best Metal Performance category with their cover of Metallica’s “Whiplash”.
  • 2015 On December, four days after his 70th birthday, Lemmy died at his Los Angeles apartment from prostate cancer, cardiac arrhythmia and congestive heart failure.
